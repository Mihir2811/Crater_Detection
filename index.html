Introduction

Lunar crater detection is essential for planetary science, space exploration, and geological studies. Traditional methods relied on manual mapping and classical image processing techniques. However, deep learning, especially YOLO-based models, has significantly improved detection efficiency and accuracy.

Traditional Methods vs. Deep Learning Approaches

Traditional Methods:

Edge Detection & Hough Transforms: Early studies used Canny Edge Detection and Circular Hough Transform (CHT) to identify crater rims.

DEM (Digital Elevation Models): LiDAR and topographic data were analyzed for crater depth and shape estimation.

Deep Learning Advances:

CNN-based Methods: Early deep learning approaches used Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), such as LeNet, VGG, and ResNet, for feature extraction.

YOLO & Object Detection Networks: More recent studies favor YOLO (You Only Look Once), Faster R-CNN, and SSD for real-time crater detection.

Model Evaluation

Training and Validation metrics

It reveals good learning and generalization losses decrease, no overfitting, high precision but lower recall, and a significant localization challenge (large gap between mAP50 and mAP50-95). Improvements are needed for recall and precise object localization.

Displaying mAP (mean Average Precision) and

loss functions (Box, Class, Object Loss) over epochs.

mAP: Increases and plateaus, suggesting the model learns but hits a performance ceiling after 200 epochs.

 

Losses: All losses (Box, Class, Object) decrease rapidly, showing the model learns. However, Box Loss fluctuates, hinting at potential instability.

In essence, the model is learning, but there's room for improvement in localization and potential training stability. Further analysis and adjustments (data augmentation, hyperparameter tuning, etc.) are needed to optimize performance.

Key findings

  • Autonomous crater mapping for lunar landers & rovers.
  • Gathering Geospatial data for lunar craters.
  • Improved planetary surface analysis for Mars & other celestial bodies.
  • Integration with satellite imaging for real-time detection.

Limitations

  • Shadows and illumination variations reduce detection accuracy.
  • False positives occur in rough terrain.
  • Small craters are harder to detect, requiring higher-resolution imagery.

Future Application

  • Planetary Exploration
  • Space Navigation & Landing Site Selection
  • Assists in hazard detection for spacecraft landing.
  • Real-time Integration with Satellite Imaging
